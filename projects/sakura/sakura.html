Thousand Cherry Blossoms - CNC'ed Object

This was made for a class called EDES 210: Prototyping and Fabrication.

“Look at the cherry blossoms as they bloom and scatter. Such is the way of life.” – Matsuo Basho

SAKURAS THOUSANDFOLD, ENTWINED WITH NIGHT

As the weather got a lot warmer, I wanted to make an object reminiscent of that fact- the cherry blossom. As such, I went on thenounproject.com in search of the perfect icon. While most of the available images seemed a bit too simple for my liking, I eventually stumbled on the following icon by Eucalyp.

However, I wasn't fond of the inside bits, and so I removed them in the slicing software.

Setting a few contour and pocket lines that I thought might be interesting, I saved my Carbide Create V8 file and reopened it in Carbide Create V7, allowing me to save that file to be opened in Carbide Create V6. In V6, I was finally able to create the GCode necessary, thus finally allowing me to use Carbide Motion to send my file over to the Shapeoko Pro. With my first attempt, I realized that I set my pockets to be too deep (the OEDK's lights were basically shining through the bottom); I feared for the structural integrity of my actual part.

Thus, after fixing the depths of my pocket cuts, I tried again. Unfortunately, due to the wood and the thickness of the outside walls, some bits inevitably cracked. That being said, I was quite pleased with the overall product.

After sanding it, I stained my pieces with the assistance of a part of a dowel.

Although I submitted the piece as is for the class, once I got it back, I knew I wanted to fill it up with pink epoxy. Mixing a 2-part High Gloss Epoxy Resin, I added red and white paint to form a vibrant pink. With this mixture, I filled the holes of my creation. Once the pink resin had dried, I poured a mix of clear resin to the top of my creation.
